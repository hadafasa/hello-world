Bears

Bear Facts

  • Bears have layers on layers of thick coats, a polar bear's coat is made up of two unique layers: The first coat provides insulation from weather, while the second larger coat prevents water from reaching the first coat layer and skin
  • Grizzly bears possess a biting force of over 1200psi, which is enough to crush a bowling ball
  • Bears are also some of the most intelligent land animals, they have the largest and most complex brains compared to other mammals their size

Giraffes

Giraffe Facts

  • Well known fact is they are the world's tallest living land animals. An adult male can grow to around 5.5m - that's taller than three adult humans!
  • Giraffes mainly eat plants. Their favourite is the acacia tree, and they use their long necks to reach the leaves and buds in the treetops. Their long tongues also help them pull down leaves growing up too high
  • They spend most of their time eating, and can eat up to 45kg of vegetation a day! Whilst they may eat a lot, giraffes don't drink much water. Most of the hydration they get is from their meals and can go days without it

Monkeys

Monkey Facts

  • Not all primates are monkeys, one big difference between monkeys and other primates is in the tail: Most monkeys have tails, while apes and other primates do not.
  • For monkeys, picking bugs, and dirt off their companions is far from just personal hygiene - its an expression of affection and love.
  • Monkeys eat far more than just bananas: Monkeys are mostly omnivorous, eating nuts, fruits, leaves, flowers, vegetables, bark, roots, rodents, birds, invertebrates, pretty much anything they can find in their habitat

Alligators

Alligator Facts

  • Alligators, along with other crocodilians, have undergone very little evolutionary change since the time of the dinosaurs. American alligators appeared about 84 million years ago, and their ancestors evolved more than 200 million years ago
  • They cannot survive in salt water, they don't have the glands to excrete salt from their bodies
  • The largest alligator ever measured was 15 feet, 9 inches long and weighed in at 1,011.5 pounds.
